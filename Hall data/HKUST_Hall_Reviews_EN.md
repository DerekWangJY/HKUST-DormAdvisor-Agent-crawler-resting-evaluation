# HKUST Undergraduate Hall Reviews (Introduction, Location, Living Experience)

This document compiles student reviews of the nine HKUST undergraduate halls — UG Hall I through UG Hall IX — covering three dimensions: Introduction, Location & Scenery, and Living Experience.

All halls of residence (including UG Hall I、 UG Hall II、 UG Hall III、 UG Hall IV、 UG Hall V、 UG Hall VI 、 UG Hall VII、 UG Hall VIII、 UG Hall IX) are unlocked and have no curfew. Residents may enter at any time simply by tapping their student card against the turnstile, in the same way as at the library. All halls of residence are equipped with lifts. The facilities in the common rooms of all halls of residence (including UG Hall I to UG Hall IX) are available at all times and have no set opening hours.

The newly built ivillage is closest to the south gate of the campus, making it convenient to take minibuses or buses to areas near MTR stations such as Choi Hung, Diamond Hill and Kwun Tong. Hall 1 is closest to the north gate of the campus, making it convenient to take minibuses or buses to areas such as Hang Hau and Tseung Kwan O.

## UG Hall I (ughall1) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall1

### UG Hall I (ughall1) - Introduction

UG Hall I is the closest hall to the academic building, approximately 300 meters away via a covered bridge. On rainy days, residents can reach the academic building without an umbrella. Although Hall I is the oldest undergraduate hall, it underwent renovation in 2015, so the overall facilities are relatively new.

### UG Hall I (ughall1) - Location & Scenery

UG Hall I is the hall of residence closest to the academic buildings and the library; it is just a 300-metre walk across the connecting bridge. As the canteen, McDonald’s, 7-Eleven and Fusion supermarket are all located in the library building, UG Hall I is also the hall of residence closest to these facilities.
It is also the highest-altitude hall among Hall I–IX. There is a small garden downstairs, and rooms on slightly higher floors with sea-facing views offer a panoramic vista of Clear Water Bay, allowing residents to enjoy sweeping views of the campus and surrounding landscape.


### UG Hall I (ughall1) - Living Experience

Hall I only has twin rooms. Each floor has two shared bathrooms/washrooms, and one floor is mixed-gender. The laundry room is located outside the hall building, equipped with a large number of washing machines and dryers, so there is no need to worry about availability even during peak laundry hours. The greatest advantage of Hall I is its proximity to the academic building — it takes only about five minutes to walk directly to the Atrium (main lobby). Students who struggle to wake up in the morning should consider Hall I as a top choice. It is also ideal for those who like to return to their hall for a nap after morning classes or grab snacks in the evening. Since the entire route to the academic building is covered, Hall I residents do not need an umbrella on rainy days — a perfect choice for those who prefer convenience. Additionally, Hall I has the oldest and most experienced hall association at HKUST, which regularly organizes a variety of fun on- and off-campus activities such as tea appreciation sessions, leather wallet-making workshops, and squid fishing trips in Sai Kung. However, Hall I rooms and Common Rooms are relatively small. The desk design that partially extends over the bed is not very friendly for taller students, so those who prefer spacious rooms or are tall may find the environment less comfortable.

## UG Hall II (ughall2) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall2

### UG Hall II (ughall2) - Introduction

UG Hall II is located at the center of the residential area. One elevator ride up leads to the LG7 canteen and Fusion; two elevator rides reach the academic building. Going down, the elevator provides direct access to the Tsang Shiu Tim Sports Center, indoor and outdoor swimming pools, Fok Ying Tung Sports Ground, and the Seafront restaurant, among other facilities. The hall has gender-separated floors, is relatively quiet, and the overall facilities are fairly new.

### UG Hall II (ughall2) - Location & Scenery

UG Hall II has well-equipped surroundings — the sports ground, restaurants, and academic building are all relatively close. A covered bridge connects directly to the academic building, so there is no need for an umbrella even on rainy days. Rooms come with either mountain or sea views, and the Common Rooms all face the sea with a direct view of the sports ground, offering excellent scenery.

### UG Hall II (ughall2) - Living Experience

UG Hall II rooms are quite spacious, especially the twin rooms, which provide ample living space. The shared bathrooms are convenient to use and are cleaned by staff daily. The Common Room is also large and equipped with a refrigerator, microwave, and other appliances. Rooms also come with a small fridge, so residents can cook during their free time. Getting to sports facilities or canteens is very convenient. The hall wardens at Hall II are very friendly and management is relatively strict, so the hall is generally quiet. The laundry room is on the 2nd floor, with 5 washing machines and 5 dryers. Whether in terms of location, room size, or overall living experience, Hall II is an outstanding and highly cost-effective choice.

## UG Hall III (ughall3) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall3

### UG Hall III (ughall3) - Introduction

UG Hall III is located further from the academic building, resembling a secluded paradise. The rooms are relatively large and the facilities are quite new, equipped with a refrigerator, wardrobe, plenty of bookshelves, and storage racks — offering very generous storage space. Half the building has sea views and the other half has mountain views. Each floor has a Common Room with sofas, coffee tables, a TV, an induction cooker, and a microwave. Twin rooms significantly outnumber triple rooms, and room types and floor assignments are randomly allocated. Three floors are mixed-gender.

### UG Hall III (ughall3) - Location & Scenery

There are two routes from the LG5 bridge to Hall III. The first requires two elevator rides, then exiting Hall II at G/F and turning left for a five-minute walk. The second requires only one elevator ride, exiting Hall I at G/F and turning left to follow a winding hillside path down to Hall III. The second route is recommended when heading to the hall — enjoy a pleasant sea breeze on the downhill walk — while the first route is better for reaching the academic building, as it avoids uphill climbing. Hall III has a garden downstairs, rooms with oversized windows offering sea or mountain views, and Common Rooms with floor-to-ceiling sea-view windows — making it one of the halls with the best scenery at HKUST.

### UG Hall III (ughall3) - Living Experience

Compared to other halls, Hall III's spaciousness, comfort, and unrivaled sea views are very attractive. It is far from the academic area and the "mosquito pond," and away from the noise of late-night parties. At night, you can open the window and fall asleep to the sound of waves without worrying about party noise disturbing you. In the morning, you can wake up to the sight of golden islands under the sunrise. The hall wardens are very friendly — most speak Mandarin — and they will remind residents if rooms are too noisy at night. The Common Rooms are very large, and each floor also has printers, TVs, fitness equipment, a table tennis table, vending machines, and an open-air balcony. Residents who enjoy cooking can purchase ingredients from nearby and use the induction cooker and microwave in the Common Room, which is more affordable and tastier than the canteen.

## UG Hall IV (ughall4) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall4

### UG Hall IV (ughall4) - Introduction

UG Hall IV has a relatively large footprint. The sea-facing side boasts stunning ocean views, while the mountain-facing side features a pond commonly known as the "mosquito pond." The overall scenery is nice, and it is convenient to reach the waterfront facilities. However, the hygiene conditions are average and the facilities are not the newest.

### UG Hall IV (ughall4) - Location & Scenery

Hall IV is relatively far from the academic building, but conveniently close to waterfront facilities and the Seafront restaurant. The football pitch and swimming pool are directly opposite, making it very easy to go out for exercise.

### UG Hall IV (ughall4) - Living Experience

Hall IV is somewhat remote but features a Common Room (CR) with beautiful sea views and a small balcony. The corridors are very long and each floor accommodates a relatively large number of residents, though the hygiene level is average. It is close to the CF restaurant and has many international students in residence. Room sizes are moderate — triple rooms feel somewhat cramped while twin rooms are more spacious, some with sea views. The beds and cupboards show some wear, in a rustic wooden tone. The hall wardens are very responsible and actively help students with questions. The G/F has a large CR and lobby, and the upper-floor CRs mostly face the sea and are much larger than those in other halls — perfect for cooking and gatherings. While Hall IV does not have a standout advantage, it also has no major drawbacks — overall a solid choice.

## UG Hall V (ughall5) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall5

### UG Hall V (ughall5) - Introduction

UG Hall V was converted from the former PG Hall II into a bunk-bed twin room hall, located on the hillside path between LG7 and Hall I (the name has never been officially changed — when ordering food delivery or calling a taxi, it is best to specify "PG Hall II" for the address). Due to its distance from other halls and its central position on the UST map, students affectionately call it the "Center of the Universe."

### UG Hall V (ughall5) - Location & Scenery

Hall V is situated along a hillside path. Walking back to Hall V offers panoramic views where the sea meets the sky, and the uphill walk doubles as exercise. Hall V has a zigzag shape, with half the rooms facing the sea and the other half facing the mountains. Although it is farther from the waterfront, making seaside facilities less immediately accessible, it is also shielded from weather effects caused by proximity to the coast.

### UG Hall V (ughall5) - Living Experience

Hall V is the only bunk-bed twin room hall. Two roommates share a large wardrobe and a long desk. The space is cozy and helps roommates bond quickly. Since the rooms were converted from single rooms, storage space is very limited, and careful planning is needed for clothing and daily essentials. Each floor has a Common Room with hot and cold drinking water, a microwave, and a shared induction cooker. On the G/F, Hall V boasts the most impressive lineup of vending machines among all halls — from instant noodles and snacks to all kinds of beverages — extremely convenient for late-night snacks or breakfast. Hall V underwent partial renovations during the summers of 2018 and 2019, so the environment is very clean and tidy, requiring only a simple clean-up upon move-in. Hall V has a total of 230 rooms, housing 460 students.

## UG Hall VI (ughall6) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall6

### UG Hall VI (ughall6) - Introduction

UG Hall VI is a highly cost-effective hall. It offers twin and triple rooms, with an in-room washbasin, and the overall facilities are relatively new. Hall VI's most notable advantage is that the late-night waterfront eatery "CF" (Seafront restaurant) is right downstairs, making it extremely popular among HKUST students.

### UG Hall VI (ughall6) - Location & Scenery

Hall VI offers convenient access to waterfront facilities and is not too far from the academic building. Rooms come with mountain or sea views (sea-view rooms are fewer), and the overall scenery is pleasant.

### UG Hall VI (ughall6) - Living Experience

Hall VI can be considered a uniquely privileged hall. Its prime location places the CF restaurant right downstairs, open until midnight — an absolute blessing for those who are too lazy to trek to LG1/LG7 canteens and for late-night snack lovers. Hall VI is also not far from the waterfront sports ground, seaside basketball court, swimming pool, and other sports facilities. While restaurant parties can sometimes be noisy at night, living on higher floors eliminates this concern entirely. Hall VI has a very convenient air-conditioning top-up system available on every floor, as well as a unique laundry monitoring system that lets residents check their laundry progress at any time. The indoor space is generous — even triple rooms do not feel cramped. Rooms have an in-room washbasin and use shared bathrooms; some floors are mixed-gender. The Common Rooms are large and tidy, with two induction cookers and two microwaves on each floor. Both the balcony and Common Room offer beautiful sea views, and at night you can even stargaze. There are several Living and Learning Communities, and the 7th floor is equipped with some sports facilities. The G/F has a wireless printer. Hall VI wardens manage things strictly — card access and visitor registration are enforced rigorously. Hall VI also organizes activities such as squid fishing trips, cycling tours around Sai Kung, seaside barbecues in Sai Kung, orientation events, and holiday celebrations — making life incredibly enriching.

## UG Hall VII - CSK Hall (ughall7) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall7

### UG Hall VII - CSK Hall (ughall7) - Introduction

Hall VII hosts a large number of international students, so its decoration and facilities are relatively new. It is often referred to as the "international student hall." Located near the sea but at a certain distance from the academic building, it has no hall association organizing activities, and the environment is relatively quiet. It also has a music room, study rooms, and other facilities available for use.

### UG Hall VII - CSK Hall (ughall7) - Location & Scenery

UG Hall VII is situated on the hillside path above the Seafront restaurant. The front side offers sea views and a view of the HKUST swimming pool, while the back faces the mountains and staff quarters. It is very convenient to reach waterfront facilities, and the distance to the academic building is similar to that of Hall VI.

### UG Hall VII - CSK Hall (ughall7) - Living Experience

Hall VII is a very clean and tidy hall, thanks to the strict management and cleaning standards enforced by its staff. For example, student organizations are not allowed to do crafts in the Common Room, and security is strict about guest entry. Hall VII's Common Rooms are spacious, with two CRs on each floor, and gatherings are frequent. Like Hall VIII and IX, Hall VII features en-suite bathrooms shared between rooms in a suite arrangement. Among the three, it has the best geographic location — relatively close to both waterfront facilities and the academic area — with beautiful sea views, though it can also be more humid. The 7th floor has a music room equipped with two pianos, an electronic keyboard, a drum set, microphones, speakers, and other music equipment. Each floor has a meeting room and a study/activity room (study room) equipped with two vending machines, 8 desks, two independent washrooms, and a whiteboard — truly the perfect combination of living, studying, and sea views! Hall VII does not have a hall association, making it relatively quiet and comfortable, but it has a Living and Learning Community (LLC) that organizes Floor Gatherings. LLC events are plentiful, and those who participate can make many new friends from diverse cultural backgrounds. On the other hand, with many international residents, it is quite common to be assigned a foreign roommate, which may involve significant differences in living habits. Overall, Hall VII offers a balanced location, a quiet and comfortable environment, and well-equipped facilities — a worthy choice.

## UG Hall VIII (ughall8) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall8

### UG Hall VIII (ughall8) - Introduction

Hall VIII & IX are the two newest dormitories before the completion of i Village, so their internal facilities are relatively new, featuring brand-new interiors and comprehensive facilities. Located at the foot of the hill near the sea, it enjoys excellent ocean views and convenient access to waterfront facilities. It also has a dedicated 24-hour study room exclusively for Hall VIII and IX residents.

### UG Hall VIII (ughall8) - Location & Scenery

Hall VIII is very conveniently located for reaching waterfront facilities, but is slightly far from the academic building — walking to LSK may take 15 to 20 minutes. One side of the hall faces the mountains while the other faces the sea; the sea-facing side also overlooks the sports ground and tennis courts, offering pleasant scenery overall.

### UG Hall VIII (ughall8) - Living Experience

Hall VIII offers one of the best living experiences and views among all halls. One side of rooms has mountain views while the other has sea views — mountain-view rooms are cooler while sea-view rooms have better ventilation. Hall VIII's most convenient feature is the suite arrangement where every two rooms share their own bathroom and shower. This is not only hygienic but also reduces foot traffic in the corridors, making it quieter. Hall VIII's facilities are quite comprehensive — there is a seaside gym just a 2-minute walk from the dormitory, and there is a study room exclusively for Hall VIII and IX residents, providing a peaceful environment for working out and studying. However, Hall VIII is far from the academic building, canteens, and supermarket, making the commute longer. Fortunately, the CF restaurant (downstairs of Hall VI) is nearby, which is very convenient for late-night snacks. Overall, Hall VIII is quiet, comfortable, and offers a pleasant and enriching lifestyle.

## UG Hall IX (ughall9) Review

- **Hall Code**: ughall9

### UG Hall IX (ughall9) - Introduction

Hall VIII and IX are the two newest halls, so the interior facilities are very new. Located at the foot of the hill, they enjoy stunning sea views and convenient access to waterfront facilities, but are quite far from the academic building. There is a gym downstairs, mainly offering fixed exercise machines, as well as a dedicated 24-hour study room exclusively for Hall VIII and IX residents.

### UG Hall IX (ughall9) - Location & Scenery

Hall IX offers very convenient access to waterfront facilities but is the farthest of all halls from the academic building. It features mountain views and expansive sea views; the sea-facing side overlooks the sports ground and tennis courts. The overall scenery is fantastic.

### UG Hall IX (ughall9) - Living Experience

The overall environment is very quiet and the hall wardens are friendly. Two twin rooms share one bathroom and one shower unit, with professional cleaning and disinfection twice a week. Rooms have in-room washbasins. Single rooms are arranged as six-person apartments, equipped with a small kitchen and a living room. If assigned a sea-view room, the living experience is beautiful and very pleasant. During more humid weather, simply turning on the air conditioning keeps the room dry. Washing machines are relatively plentiful — as long as you avoid peak hours (7–10 PM), there is generally no queue. The Common Room is not particularly large, comfortably accommodating 5–6 people. Room facilities are quite new, and although rooms may be slightly smaller than those in Hall I, II, III, or VI, this does not affect daily life.
